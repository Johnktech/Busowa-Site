Our Story

A school built by
a community, for
a community.

St. Antony Busowa Primary School was born from the conviction of Busowa Catholic Parish — that education is not a privilege but a right, and that every child in this corner of Eastern Uganda deserves a foundation for life.

From Primary 1 through Primary 7, our 25 teachers guide 1,000 children through a curriculum that prepares them not just for national exams, but for life. We are located in Busowa Town-Council, Bugiri District — 170km from Kampala along the Uganda–Kenya highway.

Our History

From mission post
to 1,000 students.

Every brick in this school has a story. Here are the milestones that shaped St. Antony Busowa Primary School.

1945
Busowa Catholic Parish Founded
The parish was established to serve the growing Catholic community of Busowa and surrounding villages, with education central to its mission from the very start.
1950s – 1970s
School Established & First Classrooms Built
St. Antony Primary School opened its doors under parish leadership, designed to accommodate 350 pupils across seven classes of 50 children each.
1990s – 2000s
Government Free Primary Education Policy
Uganda's Universal Primary Education program dramatically increased enrollment nationwide. Our school saw pupil numbers surge from 350 toward 1,000 — far exceeding original capacity.
2020s
Parish Grows to Three Schools
The parish now operates three schools across Bugiri District, serving a combined 3,000 learners — with St. Antony as the flagship institution.
2025 — Now
New Four-Classroom Block
We are building four new classrooms to decongest our overpopulated classes and restore quality education for 280 additional learners.
Mission & Values

What we stand for,
every single day.

📚
Quality Education
We believe every child, regardless of economic background, deserves a rigorous, caring, and inspiring education that prepares them for life and national examinations.
🤝
Community Service
Our school is inseparable from the Busowa community. We grow together — through parent involvement, parish leadership, and shared responsibility for every child's success.
⚖️
Human Dignity
We respect and uphold the rights of every learner. No child is left behind because of poverty, distance, or family circumstance. The school stands as a place of safety and belonging.
🌱
Environmental Care
We teach students to care for the land they live on. Tree planting, clean environment campaigns, and sustainability are woven into school life and the parish's wider mission.
🕊️
Peace & Reconciliation
In a region where ethnic and economic tensions can run high, the school and parish actively promote peace-building, dialogue, and respect across all communities.
🏗️
Accountability
Every project we undertake is supervised, reported on, and transparent. We manage donor resources with full financial accountability and photographic documentation.
