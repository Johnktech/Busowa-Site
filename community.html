Busowa Catholic Parish

More than a church.
A community anchor.

Founded in 1945, Busowa Catholic Parish has never limited its mission to worship alone. From its earliest days, the parish understood that faith without service is incomplete.

Over eight decades, the parish has built schools, supported health services, promoted human rights, championed environmental care, and worked tirelessly to reduce poverty in one of Uganda's most underserved regions.

Today the parish runs three primary schools across Bugiri District — together serving 3,000 learners — with St. Antony Busowa as the flagship institution.

What We Do

Community programs
beyond the classroom.

The parish's work touches every part of community life — from the schoolroom to the health clinic, from the courtroom to the forest.

🎓
Education
Three primary schools across Bugiri District, together serving 3,000 learners from Primary 1 through 7. The parish supplements government efforts to ensure every child in the area has access to schooling.
🏥
Health Support
Coordinating with district health services to run outreach clinics, maternal health support, and school health screenings for the most vulnerable families in the area.
🕊️
Peace Building
Active conflict mediation and inter-community dialogue programs that address ethnic and economic tensions in Bugiri District — promoting stability and social cohesion.
🌿
Environment
Reforestation campaigns, school garden programs, and environmental education integrating sustainability into the daily lives of students and families across the parish.
⚖️
Human Rights
Advocacy for the rights of children, women, and marginalized community members — including access to education, protection from child labour, and freedom from early marriage.
💰
Poverty Alleviation
Supporting families in need through school feeding programs, scholastic material assistance, and livelihood training — because a hungry child cannot learn.

"The parish has existed for since 1945. For all these years it has been doing social-religious community development work — education, health, peace building, environment, promotion of human rights."

— Busowa Catholic Parish · Bugiri District, Uganda

The Parish Schools

Three schools.
One mission.

The parish operates three primary schools in different villages of Bugiri District — each one a pillar of its community.

01
St. Antony Busowa Primary School
The flagship school, serving 1,000 learners with 25 teachers. Located on the Jinja–Tororo Road in Busowa Town-Council. Currently undertaking a four-classroom expansion project.
02
Parish School — Village 2
A second school serving a different village in Bugiri District, providing primary education to community members who would otherwise have no access to schooling.
03
Parish School — Village 3
A third school completing the parish's network across the district — together the three schools serve a combined total of 3,000 learners.
